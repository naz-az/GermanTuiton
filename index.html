Why should kids learn a new language?

Besides the usual reasons adults learn a new language (career opportunities or romance) there are plenty of other benefits for kids to learn a new language.

  • Improved memory: Learning a foreign language involves memorizing vocabulary and grammar, which enhances overall memory function. According to a study published in the journal "Neuropsychology," bilingualism has been linked to better memory performance (Bialystok et al., 2004).
  • Problem-solving skills: Language learning stimulates critical thinking and problem-solving abilities. Research published in the journal "Psychological Science" suggests that bilingual individuals excel in tasks that require cognitive control and problem-solving (Green & Abutalebi, 2013).
  • Increased creativity: Language learners often find creative ways to express themselves. Studies, such as one published in "Frontiers in Psychology," have shown that bilingualism is associated with greater cognitive flexibility and divergent thinking, key components of creativity (Kharkhurin, 2012).

Why start early

  • Kids get the opportunity to explore, do you (parents) still remember the foreign language you learnt years ago at school or at university. Chances are you won't remember much of it. Starting early gives children to explore their interests
  • Children are just better at learning stuff
  • Longer time to learn a language: The usual pathway to study in a university overseas will be to study a language intensively for 1-2 years after SPM. Starting early gives gives them the opportunity to learn at a slower pace and enjoy the process more than just cramming things in your head to pass that next exam
